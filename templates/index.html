Admission to colleges is a challenging task when a large number of students compete for limited seats, and a fair, transparent process requires allotment strictly based on rank, preferences, category and seat availability. The proposed Student Rank-Based College Allotment System is a database-driven solution that automates this process and ensures efficient, unbiased seat allocation through three main actors: Students, who register and submit their college and course preferences; Colleges, which provide information about courses and available seats; and the Allocator, a system component that processes student data, applies a rank-wise allotment algorithm, and assigns students to the highest available preference, considering subsequent preferences if a choice is filled. Students with no available preferences are marked as Not Allotted.
The implementation uses five core relational tables‚ÄîStudents, Colleges, Courses, Preferences, and Allotments‚Äîinterconnected through primary and foreign keys to maintain referential integrity, triggers to automatically update seat availability after each allotment, and views to provide quick access to allotment summaries and seat status for administrators and students. This project demonstrates the application of DBMS concepts such as entity-relationship modeling, normalization, query processing, and transaction handling, offering a scalable, efficient, and fair approach to college seat allocation, with potential enhancements including multiple allotment rounds, reservation handling, and real-time web or mobile portals.
